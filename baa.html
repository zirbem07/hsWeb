  • Definitions. The terms in this Section will have the meanings set forth below in accordance with the same meaning given to these terms by the Service Agreement or HIPAA, the HITECH Act or the HIPAA Rules, as in effect or as amended from time to time.
      1. “Services” means the Health Connection plans, features and offerings offered by Health Connection, as modified from time to time by Health Connection in its sole discretion, and selected for use by Customer at the time of enrollment and accepted by Health Connection and as then amended by Customer or Health Connection from time-to-time thereafter.
      2. “Disclosure” as defined in section 160.103 of title 45 of the Code of Federal Regulations (the “Code”), means the release, transfer, provision of access to, or divulging in any manner of information outside the entity holding the information.
      3. “Use” as defined in the Code, means, with respect to individually identifiable health information, the sharing, employment, application, utilization, examination, or analysis of such information within an entity that maintains such information.
      4. “Breach” means the definition given in section 13400(1)(A)-(B) in the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act (HITECH Act).
      5. “Data Aggregation” as defined in section 164.501 of the Code, means, with respect to protected health information created or received by a business associate in its capacity as the business associate of a covered entity, the combining of such protected health information by the business associate with the protected health information received by the business associate in its capacity as a business associate of another covered entity, to permit data analyses that relate to the health care operations of the respective covered entities.
      6. “Security Indecent” means, without limitation, pings, and other broadcast attacks on Business Associate’s firewall, port scans, unsuccessful logon attempts, denial of service attacks and any combination of the above, so long as no such incident results in unauthorized acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI.
      7. “Designated Record Set” means the definition given in section 164.501(1)(i)-(iii).
      8. “Content” means any and all information, data, and protected health information (PHI), regardless of the file format or file contents (and includes, without limitation, all photographs, text, images, audio clips, sounds, videos, databases, e-mails, computer programs, as well as any other files) uploaded by or for Customer in connection with the Customer’s use of the Services.
      9. “HIPAA” means the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, as amended, by the HITECH Act, and their implementing regulations.
      10. “HIPAA Account” means an account for Services in which Customer has indicated to us that Customer intends to provide Content containing PHI.
      11. “HIPAA Rules” means the implementing regulations found at CFR Part 160 and Part 164, as amended from time to time.
      12. “Protected Health Information: or “PHI”, as defined by the HIPAA Rules, for purposes of this BAA, means PHI that is created, received, maintained, or transmitted on behalf of Customer by Business Associate in connection with the Services performed pursuant to the Service Agreement.

This BAA also is not intended, to the full extent of applicable laws, rules and regulations, to grant any rights to any person or entity who is not a party to this BAA. This BAA only applies to the extent Business Associate is deemed a business associate to Customer under HIPAA Rules.

  • Permitted and Required Uses and Disclosures
      1. Service Offerings. Business Associate may Use or Disclose PHI for or on behalf of Customer as necessary to perform the service set forth in Service Agreement, provided that such Use of Disclosure would not violate the HIPAA Rules or HIPAA if done by a Covered Entity, as that term is defined in the HIPAA Rules.
      2. Administration and Management of Services. Business Associate may Use and Disclose PHI as necessary for the proper management and administration of Business Associate or to carry out the legal responsibilities of Business Associate, provided that the Disclosures are either required by law or Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the recipient of the PHI that: (i) the recipient will hold the PHI confidentially and will Use or Disclose the PHI only as required by law of for the purpose for which it was disclosed to the recipient, and (ii) the recipient will notify Business Associate of any instances of which it is aware in which the confidentiality of information containing PHI information has been Breached.
      3. Additional Uses and Disclosures. Unless otherwise prohibited Business Associate may Use or Disclose PHI to provide (i) as Required by Law, and (ii) Data Aggregation services requested by the Customer, where permitted under the HIPAA Rules.
      4. Minimum Necessary: Customer shall disclose to Business Associate only the minimum necessary amount of PHI for Business Associate to perform the Services and its rights and obligations under this Agreement, and only in compliance with the HIPAA Rules.
  • Obligations of Business Associate.
      1. Business Associate Obligations Conditioned. For any of Customer’s accounts that are not HIPAA Account, Business Associate does not act as a business associate under HIPAA and will have no obligations under this BAA.
      2. Limit on Uses and Disclosures. Business Associate will Use or Disclose PHI only as permitted by this BAA and Service Agreement, or as Required by Law, provided that any such use of disclosure would not violate HIPAA if done by a Covered Entity, as that term is defined in the HIPAA Rules, unless permitted under HIPAA for a Business Associate.

Business Associate will use reasonable and appropriate safeguards to prevent Use or Disclosure of the PHI other than as provided for by this BAA, consistent with the requirements of Subpart C of 45 C.F.R. Part 164 (with respect to Electronic PHI) as determined by Business Associate and as reflected in the Service Agreement.
      4. Reporting of impermissible Uses and Disclosures; Security Incidents. Business Associate will report to Customer: (i) any Use or Disclosure of PHI not permitted or required by this BAA of which Business Associate becomes aware; (ii) at least once each calendar quarter, any Security Incidents involving PHI of which Business Associate becomes aware. No further notice will be provided, and notice is hereby deemed provided, for Security Incidents.
      5. Reporting of Breaches. Business Associate will report to Customer any Breach of Customer’s unsecured PHI that Business Associate may discover to the extent required by the HIPAA Rules. Business Associate will make such report without unreasonable delay, and in no case later than 60 calendar days after discovery of such Breach.
      6. Subcontractors. Business Associate will ensure that any subcontractors that receive, maintain or transmit PHI on behalf of Business Associate agree to restrictions, conditions and requirements at least as stringent as those found in this BAA.
      7. Access to PHI; Amendment. Business Associate will make PHI in Designated Record Set available to Customer (i) as necessary for Customer to comply with 45 C.F.R. 164.524, and (ii) as necessary for Customer to amend and incorporate any amendments to the PHI in accordance with 45 C.F.R. 164.526
      8. Accounting of Disclosures. Business Associate will make available to Customer information as required to provide an accounting of Disclosures in accordance with 45 C.F.R 164.528 of which Business Associate is aware, if requested by Customer; as Business Associate cannot readily identify which Individuals are identified or what types of PHI are included in Customer’s Content which Business Associate has disclosed and for providing a brief description of the PHI disclosed.

Business Associate will make its internal practices, books, and records to the Use and Disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services for purposes of determining Customer’s compliance with HIPAA. Nothing in this section will waive any applicable protection of privilege, including trade secrets and confidential commercial information.

If the breaching party or any of its subcontractors or agents fail to timely cure the breach, the terminating party may terminate this BAA.

Where it is infeasible to return or destroy PHI, the protections of this BAA are extended to such information, in accordance with the termination provisions in this Section.

If feasible, upon termination of this BAA for any reason, Business Associate shall return to customer or destroy all PHI received from Customer, or received by Business Associate on behalf of Customer. This provision shall apply to PHI that is in the possession of subcontractors or agents of Business Associate. The parties acknowledge that it may not be feasible for Business Associate to destroy or return PHI upon termination of this BAA. If Business Associate determines that returning or destroying any PHI is not feasible, Business Associate shall notify Customer of the conditions that make return or destruction infeasible and shall extend the protections of this BAA to such PHI and limit further Uses and Disclosures of such PHI to those purposes that make the return of destruction infeasible for so long as Business Associate Maintains such PHI.
